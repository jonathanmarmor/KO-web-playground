# 7th Street Concerts — Website Audit Report

Analysis of approximately half the pages of the current 7thstreetconcerts.org website, based on
screenshots, HTML, and text captures. The site serves a Charlotte, NC nonprofit presenting
classical/chamber music in intimate settings with a strong community outreach component.

---

## Section-by-Section Analysis

---

### 1. Home Page ✅ KEEP (strengthen)

**What it contains:**
- Hero tagline: "A nonprofit concert series bringing exceptional musicians and meaningful community connection in Charlotte, NC."
- Upcoming events list with dates and brief descriptions
- "Past Community Events" photo strip (outreach photos)
- Subscribe CTA + footer with contact info and 501(c)(3) disclosure

**What works:**
- Clear value proposition immediately visible
- Dual-audience messaging: ticketed concerts + free community outreach
- Google Review testimonial ("such a joy to experience amazing music in a small, intimate space")
- QCLife pull quote about Kristin Olson and world-class performances

**What doesn't work:**
- "Past Community Events" section is a tiny photo strip with minimal context
- Upcoming events feel like a flat list rather than an engaging invitation
- No clear primary call-to-action hierarchy (tickets? donation? newsletter?)

**Recommendation:** Lead with the upcoming season's theme ("travels to Persia, from Latin America
to American roots"), feature the next concert prominently with a ticket button, then show community
impact (outreach photos + stats if available), and close with a newsletter signup.

---

### 2. About Page ✅ KEEP (simplify and restructure)

**What it contains:**
- Venue description (St. Peter's Episcopal Church, Uptown Charlotte)
- Concert format details: free parking, pre-concert happy hour, 75-min concert, post-concert reception
- Kristin Olson bio (Juilliard, USC, CalArts; founded hospital concert series at Mt. Sinai NY)
- Board Chair Rob Smith bio
- Full board member list (names only)
- Mission statement
- History (founded 2013 as Center City Concerts, went quiet during pandemic, reimagined post-COVID)
- Inline press mentions (Cultural Voice NC, Spectrum News, Art on My Sleeve, QCLife, WDAV)

**What works:**
- Kristin's backstory is compelling and mission-driven — the Mt. Sinai origin story (she started
  free hospital concerts as a thank-you after recovering from a lung injury) is a powerful hook
- The concert format (happy hour → 75-min no-intermission concert → artist reception) clearly
  differentiates 7th Street from traditional classical concerts
- The history grounds the organization with genuine continuity (10+ years in Charlotte)

**What doesn't work:**
- Board list is just names with no context — adds no visitor value
- Press mentions embedded in About make the page feel cluttered
- Rob Smith's bio is thin compared to Kristin's

**Recommendation:** Restructure: lead with mission and Kristin's story, then the concert
experience description, then history. Move press coverage to the In The News page. Board list can
be a collapsible section or omitted from the main nav entirely.

### 3. Season / Concerts Page ✅ KEEP (enhance)

**What it contains:**
- Season tagline: "travels to Persia, from Latin America to American roots—four concerts that cross
  centuries and cultures to celebrate the beauty that connects us all"
- Four concerts for 2025-26:
  1. **Luz de Navidad** (Dec 6, 2025) — Music of Spain and the Americas; benefit concert with
     food/toy drive for Nourish Up and Our Bridge for Kids
  2. **Freedom in the Air** (Jan 24, 2026) — MLK tribute; Sequina DuBose (Lyric Opera of
     Chicago, Royal Danish Opera) + Patrick Dailey (Kennedy Center)
  3. **Scenes From a Paris Cafe** (March 1, 2026) — Victor Wang (flute) + Andrea Mumm Trammell
     (harp); impressionist/salon program
  4. **The Trout Who Found the River** (April 12 & 14, 2026) — Charlotte SHOUT! free outdoor
     performances + special ticketed evening with composer conversation
- Google Review testimonial at bottom

**What works:**
- Season has a coherent narrative arc and real editorial voice
- Concert descriptions are evocative, not dry listings
- Community benefit angle (food drive, free outdoor events) is foregrounded
- Performers have verifiable top-tier credentials

**What doesn't work:**
- Individual concert pages are inconsistently built (see section 4 below)
- No performer photos on the season page
- "Learn More and Buy Tickets" links — destination not clearly labeled

**Recommendation:** Keep the narrative season overview. Standardize each concert's individual page
(see below). Consider adding performer headshots to the season listing.

### 4. Individual Concert Pages ⚠️ KEEP FORMAT (fix inconsistency)

**Ensemble Decoda page:** Date, description, reception note, ticket button. Simple but functional.
Also includes a compelling detail: the concert will feature a piece created at a "Hackathon" with
audience members credited as composers in the program — excellent storytelling.

**Gesualdo Six page:** Nearly empty (279 bytes of text captured). A credibility problem — visitors
who click through find almost nothing.

**Recommendation:** Create a reusable template for every concert page:
- Title + date/time/venue
- Evocative program description
- Performer bios with photos
- Program notes or repertoire preview
- Ticket button (prominent)
- Companion outreach event mention (if applicable)
- Post-concert reception note

Unpopulated pages should redirect to the season page rather than exist as stubs.

### 5. Gallery Page ✅ KEEP (reorganize)

**What it contains:**
- 2025-26 season: Luz de Navidad rehearsal + performance + post-concert photos (photo credit:
  Perry Tannenbaum)
- Outreach: Wingate University masterclass, Brookdale East Assisted Living performance
- 2024 inaugural season: Fire Grace & Ash, Decoda at Carolinas Medical Center, Sphinx Virtuosi +
  Charlotte Prep (Hurricane Helene Relief benefit), Gesualdo Six, International Artists
- Press quotes embedded among photos (Cultural Voice NC, WDAV, QCLife, ShareCharlotte)

**What works:**
- Photos convey both the concert quality and the community mission simultaneously
- Embedded press quotes add credibility throughout the scroll
- Outreach photos (hospital, assisted living, university masterclass) are mission-critical content
  that builds donor and funder confidence

**What doesn't work:**
- Minimal captions — hard to identify performers or venues
- No organization by event or year — one enormous scroll
- Outreach and concert photos mixed without distinction

**Recommendation:** Reorganize with filters or tabs: **Current Season | Past Seasons | Community
Outreach**. Add captions consistently (who, where, when). Standardize photo credits.

### 6. Donate Page ✅ KEEP (expand)

**What it contains:**
- "Donate Now" button (external processor)
- Brief mention of sponsorship opportunities
- Reference to contact Kristin for more information

**What works:**
- Simple and frictionless for individual donors

**What doesn't work:**
- No emotional hook or impact language
- Sponsorship content is just a mention; the real tiers are on the separate Partners page (confusing split)
- No suggested donation amounts or giving levels
- No explanation of what donations fund

**Recommendation:** Merge Donate + Partners into a unified "Support Us" page with two subsections:
individual giving and corporate sponsorship. Add impact framing: "Your donation supports free
outreach performances at hospitals, shelters, and schools." Add a recurring giving option.

### 7. Partners Page ✅ KEEP (merge with Donate)

**What it contains:**
Four corporate sponsorship tiers:
- **Program Sponsor — $100:** Full-color ad in concert program + social media recognition
- **Reception Sponsor — $500:** Brand visibility at reception, banner/materials, social media,
  complimentary tickets
- **Outreach Sponsor — $1,000:** Logo on program cover, outreach event presence, verbal
  acknowledgment, social media, comp tickets
- **Concert Sponsor — $1,500:** Premier logo on program cover, concert presence, stage
  acknowledgment, social media, comp tickets
- Note: custom sponsorships available
- Partner logo grid (appears empty in capture)

**What works:**
- Tiers are clearly differentiated
- Tier names map directly to the concert experience (Program, Reception, Outreach, Concert)
- "Custom sponsorships available" is a smart hedge

**What doesn't work:**
- Separated from Donate — sponsors visiting the Donate page won't find this
- Partner logo section appears blank — current partners are not being showcased
- No existing-sponsor testimonial about the value of the partnership

**Recommendation:** Merge into "Support Us." Display partner logos prominently. A quote from an
existing sponsor would strengthen the pitch.

### 8. Join / Mailing List Page ✅ KEEP (integrate everywhere)

**What it contains:**
- Email, first name, last name fields
- Subscribe button

**What works:** Simple, low-friction.

**What doesn't work:**
- Exists as an isolated page rarely visited organically
- No value proposition — why subscribe? What do subscribers get?

**Recommendation:** Embed the form in the footer of every page (the current site already uses a
"SUBSCRIBE" footer link — build on this) and prominently on the homepage. Add a pitch: "Be the
first to know about concerts, artist announcements, and ticket presales." Keep the standalone page
for direct links from social ads.

### 9. In The News Page ⚠️ KEEP (expand significantly)

**What it contains:**
- Four outlet links: Spectrum News, Reader's Picks: Favorite Local Charities, Art on My Sleeve,
  QCLife

**What works:** External validation and credibility.

**What doesn't work:**
- Extremely sparse — outlet name and link only, no headline, date, quote, or context
- WDAV and Cultural Voice NC appear in the Gallery and About pages but are absent here
- Visitors have no reason to click without knowing what the articles say

**Recommendation:** Treat this as real social proof. For each piece of coverage: show the
publication name/logo, article headline, publication date, a pull quote or excerpt, and a link.
Group by year. This page should make visitors feel the organization is legitimately covered, not
just connected to a few links.

### 10. Thank You Page ✅ KEEP (add next steps)

**What it contains:**
- "Thank you for your donation. Your gift goes directly to support 7th Street Concerts and helps
  to bring music to our community that's fresh, classical, a little unexpected."

**What works:** Warm, on-brand, genuine language.

**What doesn't work:** No next step — a highly engaged visitor lands here and has nothing to do.

**Recommendation:** Add: newsletter signup CTA, social follow prompt, link to upcoming concerts,
and optionally social share buttons ("Share with friends who love live music").

## Features to ADD in the New Website

### A. Ticketing Integration (High Priority)
The current ticket purchase flow is unclear. Concert pages should display pricing, availability,
and link to the purchase flow with a clear, prominent button. Even with a third-party processor
(Eventbrite, etc.), the handoff should be seamless and trust-building.

### B. Artist/Performer Profiles
Performers like Sequina DuBose, Patrick Dailey, and Victor Wang are mentioned but have no photos
or bios on the site. A brief spotlight (headshot, 2-3 sentences, notable credentials) on each
concert page would help audiences connect and make ticket purchases feel personal.

### C. Community Outreach Impact Section
The outreach mission — free performances at hospitals, shelters, and schools for every ticketed
concert — is undersold. A dedicated section or page showing: number of outreach performances,
venues served (Carolinas Medical Center, Roof Above, Mercy Hospital, Brookdale East, Wingate
University), photos, and participant/staff quotes would strengthen donor appeals and grant
applications significantly.

### D. Season Archive / Past Seasons
The 2024 inaugural season content is scattered across the gallery with no organized home. A "Past
Seasons" section (or archive within the Season page) documenting each year's concerts, artists,
and highlights builds institutional credibility and lets new audiences explore the history.

### E. Upcoming Events Calendar
A simple event list or calendar widget on the homepage showing all upcoming performances —
including free outreach events — helps visitors plan. It also signals that the organization is
active and has a full schedule.

### F. Accessibility Information
No current mention of ADA accessibility at St. Peter's Episcopal Church, accessible parking,
large-print programs, or hearing loop availability. Classical music audiences skew older — this
information matters and signals a welcoming organization.

### G. Social Media Links
The site has newsletter Subscribe CTAs everywhere but no visible social media links. Instagram in
particular is a natural fit for concert photography and artist spotlights. Links should appear in
the header or footer persistently.

### H. Volunteer / Get Involved Opportunities
A simple "Get Involved" section (volunteer, bring a group, suggest an outreach venue, host a
post-concert reception) could convert casual visitors into active community members beyond just
donors.

---

## What to LEAVE OUT

### 1. Board Member List (Names Only)
The current board list — just names — adds no value to a visitor. Options: give board members
brief bios explaining their connection to the mission (like Rob Smith's does), or move this
information entirely off the main site.

### 2. Sparse Individual Concert Pages
Concert pages with almost no content (like the Gesualdo Six page) damage credibility. In the new
site, either populate every individual concert page fully using the template above, or don't
create them until they're ready — redirect stubs to the season overview page.

### 3. Standalone Join Page as Primary CTA
The `/join` page works as a landing destination for external links, but shouldn't be a main nav
item competing with higher-priority destinations. Embed signup forms throughout the site instead.
