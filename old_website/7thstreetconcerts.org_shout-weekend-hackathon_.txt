Music + Technology Hackathon

April 5, Noon–8:00 p.m.
CALLING ALL MUSICIANS, COMPOSERS, TECHNOPHILES, CREATIVE TYPES.

The Music + Technology Hackathon is a free, collaborative event where you can attend workshops with musicians, composers and technology experts, then join breakout sessions where you can collaborate and explore creative projects with professional mentors. The day wraps up with a final concert featuring all the work produced during the day. Registration required.

Register for the Music Hackathon
The Hackathon is led by Jonathan Marmor, Director of Engineering at Spotify. Teresa Nakra, Director of Music and Technology at the Stevens Institute of Technology, near New York City, known for its focus on technology and innovation. Kevin Farrell, composer, bassists and music educator at Calhoun – an independent school in Manhattan. Eric Rosenbaum, founder of Makey-Makey, an incredible tech toy that turns everyday objects into touchpads. Named one of Consumer Reports’ Best Tech Toys of 2014 and finalist for Toy of the Year, 2016.

Decoda, an artist-led collective that seeks to create a more compassionate and connected world through music, will present on performing with technology and collaborating with composers.

Backspinz, a leading music academy for students aiming for careers in music and entertainment.
